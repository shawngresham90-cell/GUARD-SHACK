Read this first. No app can dig deleted files out of a locked phone — that's a built-in safety feature of your phone, not something an app can bypass. But the spots below actually hold your deleted items, often for 30–60 days. Check them now.

🍎 iPhone recovery most likely to work

Try these in order. Photos and texts have separate trash folders.

1
Photos → Albums → Recently DeletedKept up to 30 days. Tap a photo → Recover.
2
Messages → Edit/Filters → Recently DeletediOS 16+ keeps deleted texts ~30 days. Select → Recover.
3
Settings → [your name] → iCloud → PhotosIf iCloud backup was on, missing photos may still be in iCloud.com.
4
iCloud.com → Account Settings → Restore FilesOn a computer, Apple keeps some recently deleted files for 30 days.

🤖 Android recovery most likely to work

Steps vary slightly by brand (Samsung, Pixel, etc.).

1
Google Photos → Library → TrashKept up to 60 days. Tap photo → Restore.
2
Samsung: Gallery → ☰ → TrashSamsung keeps deleted photos ~30 days separately.
3
Google Messages → archive / Recycle binCheck Archived. Samsung Messages → ⋮ → Recycle bin.
4
Google One / BackupSettings → Google → Backup. Restore can bring back synced data.

⚠️ Avoid the scams

Most “recover deleted photos” apps in the app stores can’t actually work and may steal your data. Stick to the built-in trash folders above. For deep recovery, only a trusted computer tool (EaseUS, Dr.Fone, PhoneRescue) with your phone plugged in has any real chance.
