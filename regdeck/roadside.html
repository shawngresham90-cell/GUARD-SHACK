🚨 ROADSIDE MODE

Pulled over by DOT? Hit this first.

Step 1 — Stay Calm Hands on wheel. Window down. Be respectful. You're not in trouble until you make yourself trouble. Shut the truck off ONLY if asked.
📋 Documents to Have Ready
  • CDL (with current medical card)
  • Logbook / ELD print-out (last 8 days)
  • Bill of Lading / shipping papers
  • Registration & insurance (cab card)
  • DVIR (last driver's inspection report)
  • Annual inspection sticker on trailer
🎯 Top 5 Regs DOT Will Cite
§395.8
Driver's Record of Duty Status (Logs)
▶

What it says

You must keep an accurate record of duty status for every 24-hour period. ELD or paper log must show all 4 statuses with location, date, and time.

What DOT looks for

Missing entries. Wrong duty status. No location. Form & manner violations. Driving while logged off-duty.

Shawn's Take — 17 yrs, 0 violations Don't argue your log on the side of the road. Hand it over clean. If something's off, you fix it on paper LATER — never in front of the officer.
§396.11
Driver Vehicle Inspection Report (DVIR)
▶

What it says

Driver must prepare a written DVIR at the end of each driving day for every vehicle operated, covering specific components.

What DOT looks for

Missing DVIR. Pre-trip not done. Defects not reported. Driving after a defect was found and not fixed.

Shawn's Take DOT will ask "did you do your pre-trip?" If you say yes and your DVIR is blank — you just lied on a federal form. Always log it. Always.
§392.2
Driving Rules (State Laws)
▶

What it says

Every CMV driver must obey the traffic laws of the jurisdiction they're driving in.

What DOT looks for

Speeding. Following too close. Lane violations. Cell phone use. Seatbelt off. Texting.

Shawn's Take §392.2 is the catch-all. Anything you'd get a ticket for in a 4-wheeler, you get it worse in a truck. Phone down. Seatbelt on. Every mile.
§391.41
Medical Qualifications
▶

What it says

Driver must be medically certified and carry a current medical examiner's certificate (long form or wallet card).

What DOT looks for

Expired medical card. No card on person. Medical condition not declared. Self-cert mismatch with state.

Shawn's Take Check your med card expiration the same day you check your insurance. Set a phone reminder 60 days out. Expired med card = out of service.
§393 (series)
Equipment / Parts & Accessories
▶

What it says

Covers lights, brakes, tires, mirrors, horn, windshield, load securement — everything mechanical on the truck and trailer.

What DOT looks for

Lights out. Tire tread < 4/32 (steer) or 2/32 (drive/trailer). Air leaks. Brake issues. Bad mud flaps. Loose load securement.

Shawn's Take 90% of out-of-service violations come from §393. Walk around your truck like you OWN it — because that violation goes on YOUR record, not the company's.
✅ What to SAY
  • "Yes sir / Yes ma'am"
  • "My CDL and medical card are right here"
  • "My logs are on my ELD — let me pull them up"
  • "I completed my pre-trip this morning at [time]"
  • "Thank you officer"
❌ What NOT to Say
  • "I don't know" (admits lack of knowledge)
  • "My dispatcher told me to..." (no excuse)
  • "I always do it this way" (admits pattern)
  • "Am I in trouble?" (don't ask)
  • Anything sarcastic. Anything.
  • Don't argue the regulation on the road
