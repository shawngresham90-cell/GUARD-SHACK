DOT Inspection Cheat Sheet

Print this. Fold it. Keep it in your cab.
Built by Shawn Gresham — 17 years driving · 0 violations · CDL Instructor at Rosedale Transport · truckinglifewithshawn.com

⚡ Before the officer walks up (30 seconds)

  1. Turn off your phone. Face down. Don't be on a call.
  2. Have your stuff ready. CDL · medical card · registration · insurance · logbook/ELD · permit book — in your hand BEFORE they reach your door.
  3. Cab clean. No open containers. No clutter on the dash. No CB blaring.

✅ The 12 things DOT checks — know these cold

📋 Documents
  • CDL — current, correct class, endorsements valid
  • Medical card — not expired (most common violation)
  • Registration — current, in the truck
  • Insurance card — current, matches the truck
  • Permit book — IFTA, IRP, fuel tax, oversize if applicable
  • Hazmat papers — placards match shipping papers (if hauling)
🚛 Logs
  • ELD displaying correctly — last 7 days available, no errors
  • Log current to last duty change (if paper)
  • Supporting docs — BOLs, receipts match log entries
🛠 Equipment
  • Tires — no flats/cuts; 4/32" steers, 2/32" others
  • Lights — ALL working: head, brake, turn, marker, clearance
  • Brakes — in adjustment, no leaks, ABS light off

💬 What to say (and not say)

✅ Say this:
  • "Yes sir / yes ma'am."
  • "Here's everything you asked for."
  • "I just did my pre-trip — last stop was [town]."
  • Short. Direct. Respectful. Eye contact.
❌ Don't say this:
  • "I'm in a hurry."
  • "Why am I being stopped?" (Already lost.)
  • "My company / dispatcher told me…" (You're responsible — not them.)
  • "I don't know."
  • Any cussing. Any sarcasm. Any attitude.
The officer decides if you roll or sit. Make their job easy.

🚨 5 common OOS triggers — fix before leaving the yard

  1. Out-of-adjustment brakes — #1 reason for OOS. Pre-trip them every single time.
  2. Flat / under-inflated tire — visual + thump test every stop.
  3. Expired medical card — set a phone alarm 60 days before expiration.
  4. HOS violation in the last 7 days — the ELD will tell on you. Don't drive over hours.
  5. Hazmat placard mismatch — papers MUST match what's on the truck.
Any one of these = you're parked. And it goes on your CSA score.

🛡 If you get a violation

  1. Sign it. Refusing = arrest in some states. Signing is NOT admitting guilt.
  2. Read it before you leave the scale. Officer wrote the wrong thing? Address it NOW.
  3. Photograph every page of the inspection report.
  4. Don't argue at the scale. Fight it in court or DataQ if you need to.
  5. Write down everything within 30 minutes: officer name, badge #, time, location, weather, what was said.
