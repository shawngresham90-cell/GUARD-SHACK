🚦 Before You Move

Is this move legal? Answer honest, get a straight answer — before the ELD and the DOT answer it for you.
Property-carrying, US interstate rules (49 CFR 395.3). Use your ELD numbers. When in doubt, use the worse number.

1 · Hours DRIVEN since your last 10-hour break

Total driving time since you last had 10 consecutive hours off (or an equivalent split).

2 · Hours since you CAME ON DUTY today

Clock time since you first went on duty after that 10-hour break — this is your 14-hour window. Breaks don't stop it.

3 · Hours driven since your last 30-minute break

Any 30-minute non-driving stretch counts (off duty, sleeper, or on-duty not driving).

4 · Your cycle

Which cycle does your carrier run, and how many on-duty hours have you burned in it?

5 · Adverse driving conditions?

Weather or a crash you could NOT have known about when you were dispatched. Normal rush hour is NOT adverse.
